Los Angeles Theatre

The balcony level oversees the the magnificent Los Angeles Theatre stage. Combined, the Mezzanine and Balcony level seat 648 attendees. This floor also includes a then state-of-the-art projection booth to provide the best film quality to its visitors. Click on the different sections floor for more information.

The Mezzanine level contained two “crying rooms”, where mothers can take their babies to watch the show in a glassed booth that contains its own speaker, air conditioning controls and a restroom.

In Richard Attenborough’s Chaplin, we get to see Chaplin (Robert Downey Jr.) and Oona O’Neil Chaplin (Moira Kelly) on the theater’s balcony.

The projection booth is located a floor above the balcony, and is the highest room in the theater.

The projection equipment used cutting-edge technology and consisted of a large booth that was able to hold extra projectors in case of one breaking down. It also included two large spotlights and a machine called the Brenograph that would project song slides and announcements on the screen. The projection booth also held a unique periscope system that used a prism and a series of large mirrors to project the film image from the booth down to the basement lounge, allowing the people below to see the screen.

One little known trivia about the projection booth is that it also served as a filming location for a couple of films, most notably Steven Spielberg’s 1941, and David Fincher’s Fight Club.
