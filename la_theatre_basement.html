Los Angeles Theatre

The basement floor was designed as entertainment for the next crowd that was waiting for the next show. There were multiple entertainment spaces to keep the crowd happy and excited before the next show such as children’s playroom, refreshment room with a soda fountains, marvelous restrooms. Click on the different sections for more information. Interestingly, the Music Room as shown on the blueprint was never built so we haven’t been able to find what the room was intended for. The one clue is that blueprint shows a lot of screens. What do you think it was meant for?

This is the unbuilt music room. Unfortunately, not much is known about it because it was never built.

The basement lounge served as an additional waiting area for patrons waiting for the next show. Ladies would mingle with the men in the walnut-panelled lounge, where a periscope-like system of prisms relayed the featured film from the auditorium to a secondary screen.

We see the basement lounge featured in Rush Hour 3, when Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker’s characters enter the Parisian casino.

  • Rush Hour 3, 2007
  • New York, New York, 1977

In the Ladies room, each stall uses a different type of marble. The Ladies room was also one of the first places to have hand dryers. In the Cosmetic Room, there were mirrors, chairs, and tables for females to freshen up including a three-sided full-length mirror. Ladies would congregate in a glamorous restroom featuring sixteen private compartments, each finished in a different marble.

The ladies room and cosmetics room are featured in Neon Demon. Here we can see the different marble and the lavish decor in the restroom.

  • Neon Demon, 2016

The Children’s room was very bright and was filled with fun toys and even swings. According to the blueprint, the room was intended to have a mirror section so that the children could see what was happening on the actual stage. However, this was never built.
